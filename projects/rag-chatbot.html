AI chatbot with RAG architecture

A retrieval-augmented chatbot that answers questions in English, grounded entirely in a corpus of French-language football articles, with added query rewriting and relevance filtering.

ML & NLP Python

The source material

The chatbot is grounded in a pre-built vector store of articles from Les Cahiers du Football, a French outlet known for analytical writing on the sport — its history, tactics, and its relationship to media and business, rather than match reports.

~3,000source articles
~20,000embedded chunks
FR → ENretrieval to response

What it had to do

The assignment set a clear baseline, on top of which groups could add complexity:

  • ✓ Accept questions in English and answer in English, despite the underlying documents being in French.
  • ✓ Return the titles of the source articles behind any answer, not just the answer text.
  • ✓ Recognise when no document is a good match, and say so honestly rather than guessing.

I went past this baseline by adding query rewriting (reformulating the user's question before retrieval to improve match quality) and a relevance-filtering step that reviews retrieved chunks before they're used to generate an answer, rather than passing every retrieved chunk straight through.

Example queries

The assignment specified two test cases that illustrate the system's range — one squarely answerable, one clearly out of scope:

In scope

"Summarise in bullet points the point of view on the usage of video-assisted refereeing."

→ Retrieves relevant chunks and answers directly.

Out of scope

"Explain the concept of manifest destiny."

→ Recognised as unrelated to football, correctly declines with "I don't know."

What I'd do differently

I'd add the metadata-filtered search — letting a user ask something like "in articles published in March 2016..." and have the retriever actually use that date filter, rather than relying on semantic similarity alone.
